Flight Validated

Waterloo Rocketry Recovery System

SolidWorksGD&TMachiningDFMAFlight Hardware

Waterloo Rocketry · Jan 2026 – Present

The first problem I worked on was a staged deployment failure where the drogue never came out. I traced it back to the shear pins not breaking because the black powder charge pressure was too low. I increased the BP load, filed the charge tube walls to lower the shear threshold, and ran ground tests until we had clean separation consistently. We then validated it in a full test flight with successful drogue, pilot, and main deployment.

On the hardware side, I designed the L-bracket mounting interfaces and the threaded hole patterns for the load-bearing aluminum bulkheads. I produced GD&T-annotated fabrication drawings and then machined and hand-tapped the components myself from aluminum stock on the mill and drill press, working directly from my own drawings to tolerance.

In Progress

Vacuum Chamber Sub-System

Pressure Vessel DesignSolidWorksInstrumentationDFMA

Waterloo Rocketry · Mar 2026 – Present

The issue was that our black powder charges are strong enough to blow the lid off a standard container, so I designed a proper vacuum sub-chamber from scratch. I sized it for energetic pressure loads up to 5g BP with a safety factor of 5, designed an O-ring face-seal and venting mechanism that holds vacuum even through initiator firing, and instrumented it with a pressure transducer for real-time pressure-time data acquisition.

The goal is to characterize how our initiators actually perform at simulated high-altitude pressures so we stop guessing at competition altitude. I'm also bringing a junior team member through the fabrication and assembly process, walking them through the drawings and the constraints behind each decision.

Adaptive Cruise Control Scaled Demonstrator

ArduinoSolidWorksElectronicsFDMMechatronics

Independent Project · Apr 2026 – Present

I started this project because I wanted to understand how adaptive cruise control actually works at the systems level. So far I've built the sensing and response logic — ultrasonic distance sensing with a three-zone LED indicator that switches between green, yellow, and red based on following distance in real time, with a servo already integrated for physical braking response.

Right now I'm designing the full mechanical system in SolidWorks: the chassis, mounting hardware, and braking mechanism, all going to FDM fabrication. The goal is a complete end-to-end system, electronics through physical hardware, that I built and understand fully.

1 Week

Stirling Engine Kinematic Assembly

SolidWorksGD&TKinematicsV&V

Independent Project · Feb 2026

I built this one week in February because I wanted to actually understand how a Stirling engine works, and modeling it seemed like the most direct way to find out. I put together a fully constrained alpha-cycle assembly, added GD&T callouts for the piston-crank interfaces, and ran interference analysis across the full range of motion to confirm there was zero contact at any operating configuration.

I finished it in one week while carrying a full course load. It is the kind of project I do just to learn something properly.
